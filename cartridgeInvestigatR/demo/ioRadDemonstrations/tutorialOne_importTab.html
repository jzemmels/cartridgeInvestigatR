Tutorial 1: Import Tab

6 STEPS

1. After opening cartridgeInvestigatR, click 1. Preprocessing

2. Click Select a folder containing x3p files

3. Select a folder containing x3p files to upload

4. After selecting a folder, click Select to upload the scans

5. If the uploaded scans are already preprocessed (they are not in the example shown), then click Skip Automatic Preprocessing.

6. Otherwise, if the scans require automatic preprocessing, proceed to the Automatic Preprocess tab.

Tutorial 2: Automtaic Preprocess Tab

22 STEPS

1. After uploading x3p scans that require preprocessing, click on the Automatic Preprocess tab.

2. Add sequential preprocessing steps by clicking Add another preprocessing step an arbitrary number of times.

3. Select a preprocessing step from the drop-down menu.

4. If you select Crop, then choose which Region of the scan you wish to crop and an Offset value to change the amount of cropping.

5. Select a Region from the dropdown.

6. Selecting Exterior will crop the outside of the cartridge case primer.

7. Enter a number into the Offset field to change the amount of cropping (a negative value will remove more of the primer observations from the scan when Region is set to "Exterior").

8. Continue adding preprocessing steps by selecting Add another preprocessing step

9. Selecting Interior for a Crop preprocessing step will remove observations in the firing pin impression region.

10. A positive value will remove more of the primer observations from the scan when Region is set to "Interior".

11. The Level preprocessing step removes the global trend from a scan by subtracting an estimated conditional statistic (Median or Mean).

12. Select a conditional Statistic to estimate and remove in the scan.

13. A Filter preprocessing step applied a Gaussian filter

14. Select a type of Gaussian filter (a bandpass or lowpass).

15. Enter number(s) into the Wavelengths field. Use one value for a lowpass filter and two comma-separated values for a bandpass filter.

16. A Downsample preprocess step reduces the dimension of the scan.

17. Enter a whole number into the Stride field. Larger values reduce the size of scan more.

18. Once you're happy with the preprocessing steps, click Perform Automatic Preprocessing. Preprocessing may take a few minutes to complete.

19. A visualization of the preprocessed scans will appear at the bottom of the page. Inspect this plot to determine whether alternative or further processing is required.

20. If you're happy with the preprocessing performed, you may continue onto the Comparing stage.

21. If would like to change the automatic preprocessing steps, click the Reset button to start over.

22. Continue to the Manual Deletion tab if you wish to manually remove regions of the scan.

Tutorial 3: Manual Deletion Tab

12 STEPS

1. Whether you perform preprocessing in the Automatic Preprocessing tab or upload scans that are preprocessed, you may wish to manually remove certain regions of a scan. In the example shown, we wish to remove the highlighted dark purple region.

2. Click on the Manual Deletion tab.

3. Select a scan from the dropdown.

4. In this case, we are interested in removing a region from x3p3.

5. A plot will appear of the selected scan.

6. With your mouse, click and drag to highlight a region of interest.

6b. Drop

7. A zoomed-in visualization of the region will appear below.

8. Click on the visual to place a point.

9. Place three or more points to start a polygon. This polygon will be removed from the scan. Continue placing points until the region of interest is highlighted. In the example show, we will highlight the dark purple region.

10. Once the region of interest is fully highlighted, click Confirm Annotations.

11. Inspect plot that appears to determine whether additional deletion is needed.

12. Once you're happy with the manual deletions, you may continue on to the Comparing stage.
